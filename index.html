EDITORIAL

Every Engineer Has A Story.
Mine Happens To Be Written In Code.

Sunday Feature • Engineering Edition

Technology never fascinated me because it was complicated. It fascinated me because it could simplify the complicated.

During college, hackathons introduced me to problems that couldn't be solved by writing a few functions or building another CRUD application. They demanded systems capable of reasoning, collaboration and adaptability.

That curiosity evolved into building enterprise AI solutions where Large Language Models interact with structured databases, Knowledge Graphs, Retrieval-Augmented Generation pipelines and autonomous agents.

"The best software isn't the one with the most code. It's the one people trust."

Today my work focuses on designing intelligent systems that help engineers make decisions faster, automate repetitive operations and transform unstructured information into actionable knowledge.

This newspaper isn't simply a portfolio. It's an archive documenting experiments, failures, victories, hackathons, deployments and lessons collected throughout my engineering journey.

— Manan Jain
